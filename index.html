Why London?

London, the city of unique and magnificent landmarks

London is probably the only one city that has emerged from every single period in its two thousand years of history, more impressive, more diverse and more generously endowed with the gems of a remarkable civilisation.It has a very diverse collection, from hundred years old castles and cathedrals to unique galleries and cultural hubs, from famous sights, easily recognisable all over the world to special hidden gems that pop up when you least expect it.

World-renowned architects, like Norman Foster (The Gherkin), Renzo Piano (The Shard), John Nash (Regent Street) or the likes of Christopher Wren (St Paul's Cathedral), John Soane (Bank of England) or Charles Barry (Houses of Parliament), have put their incredible talent to great use to create some of the most striking designs that have shaped the London landscape and skyline as we know it today.
